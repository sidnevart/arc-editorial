# Post 02: the bottleneck was runtime, not the model

## Copy

The more I work with AI tooling for engineering, the less I believe the answer is simply “pick a stronger model”.

Once you try to scale usage beyond a few enthusiasts, it becomes obvious:

the bottleneck is usually not model intelligence.
It is runtime discipline.

What actually matters:

- how context is assembled
- how behaviours are separated by mode
- how facts, decisions and open questions are stored
- how outputs are verified
- how the handoff between human and agent is structured

This is why I increasingly think of AI dev tools as engineering systems, not smart chats.

An unmanaged runtime gives you impressive chaos.
A well-structured runtime gives you something teams can actually rely on.
